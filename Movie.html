All Movies

BRAVE: offers young audiences and fairy tale fans a rousing, funny fantasy
adventure with a distaff twist and surprising depth.

MONSTERS UNIVERSITY: Offering Monsters, Inc. fans a return visit with beloved
characters, Monsters University delivers funny and thoughtful family entertainment
for viewers of any age.

RATATOUILLE: Fast-paced and stunningly animated, Ratatouille adds another delightfully
entertaining entry -- and a rather unlikely hero -- to the Pixar canon.

COCO:Coco's rich visual pleasures are matched by a thoughtful narrative that takes a
family-friendly -- and deeply affecting -- approach to questions of culture, family,
life, and death.

TOY STORY 4: Heartwarming, funny, and beautifully animated, Toy Story 4 manages
the unlikely feat of extending -- and perhaps concluding -- a practically perfect animated saga.

JEXI:It's hard to tell whether the lack of laughs in Jexi is a bug or a feature, but
this AI rom-com is sorely in need of an OS update.

THE HUSTLE:The Hustle's stars might make an effective comedy team in a different setting,
but this gender-flipped remake of a remake adds little beyond its feminine twist.

BEING FRANK:Being Frank suggests Jim Gaffigan may have a real future as a dramatic actor
if only he's offered projects that don't suffer from so much wasted potential.

ISN'T IT ROMANTIC:It follows as many genre conventions as it mocks, but Isn't It Romantic is
a feel-good rom-com with some satirical bite -- and a star well-suited for both.

SINK OR SWIM (LE GRAND BAIN):A talented cast and a pleasantly bittersweet spin on a familiar
story help Sink or Swim finish a length or two ahead of the average feel-good comedy.
